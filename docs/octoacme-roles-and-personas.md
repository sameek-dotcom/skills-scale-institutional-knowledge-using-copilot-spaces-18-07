# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Stakeholder Liaison / Relations Manager

### Role Summary
The Stakeholder Liaison manages communication channels between the project team and external/internal stakeholders. This role ensures stakeholder expectations are aligned with project realities and serves as the primary escalation point for stakeholder concerns.

### Responsibilities
- Manage stakeholder communications and expectation setting
- Facilitate regular stakeholder updates and status reviews
- Serve as the primary escalation point for stakeholder concerns
- Gather stakeholder feedback and requirements
- Document and communicate project status to leadership
- Coordinate with delivery teams on stakeholder-requested clarifications
- Manage stakeholder engagement activities and feedback loops

### Goals
- Maintain high stakeholder satisfaction and engagement
- Ensure alignment between stakeholder expectations and project delivery
- Minimize stakeholder-driven scope creep and rework
- Improve transparency and trust across organizational boundaries

### Key Interactions
- **With Project Manager**: Provides stakeholder feedback, escalates concerns, coordinates status reporting
- **With Executive Sponsor**: Ensures senior leadership alignment, escalates critical issues
- **With Delivery Teams**: Clarifies stakeholder requirements, gathers technical feasibility feedback
- **With Product Manager**: Aligns stakeholder needs with product priorities

### Escalation Path
- Routine concerns → Project Manager
- Critical/urgent issues → Executive Sponsor
- Cross-functional conflicts → Project Manager + relevant department leads

### Typical Activities
- Conducting stakeholder interviews and discovery sessions
- Preparing and presenting executive briefings
- Managing feedback collection and prioritization
- Coordinating stakeholder review sessions
- Documenting and communicating decisions impacting stakeholders

---

## Quality Assurance Owner

### Role Summary
The Quality Assurance Owner develops and oversees the testing strategy, establishes quality standards, and manages defect identification and resolution. This role ensures deliverables meet required quality thresholds before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define testing strategy and test plans for each phase
- Establish quality standards and acceptance criteria
- Oversee test execution and defect management
- Report quality metrics and trends to leadership
- Collaborate with developers on test coverage and automation
- Manage quality escalations and blocked releases
- Plan and execute regression testing for each release

### Goals
- Ensure consistent quality standards across all deliverables
- Minimize defects in production environments
- Reduce testing cycle time through automation and efficiency
- Maintain high test coverage and defect detection rates

### Key Interactions
- **With Developers**: Reviews code quality, defines test requirements, coordinates test automation
- **With Project Manager**: Reports quality metrics, manages release readiness assessments
- **With Product Manager**: Validates acceptance criteria alignment with quality standards
- **With Stakeholder Liaison**: Communicates quality status and any release risks to stakeholders

### Escalation Path
- Quality concerns → Project Manager
- Release-blocking defects → Project Manager + Executive Sponsor
- Systematic quality issues → Product Manager + Developers

### Typical Activities
- Creating and maintaining test plans and test cases
- Defining test automation strategy
- Running test cycles and reporting results
- Triaging and tracking defects
- Conducting quality reviews and readiness assessments
- Documenting test coverage metrics

## Compliance & Risk Officer

### Role Summary
The Compliance & Risk Officer ensures the project adheres to regulatory requirements, organizational policies, and risk management standards. This role identifies, assesses, and mitigates project risks while ensuring compliance with relevant governance frameworks.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct compliance and regulatory impact assessments
- Identify and assess project risks and dependencies
- Develop risk mitigation and contingency plans
- Review deliverables for compliance with organizational policies
- Maintain risk register and compliance documentation
- Provide guidance on regulatory and policy implications
- Monitor risk metrics and escalate critical risks

### Goals
- Ensure project operates within regulatory and compliance boundaries
- Minimize unplanned risks and compliance violations
- Maintain organizational reputation and governance integrity
- Enable informed decision-making through risk transparency

### Key Interactions
- **With Project Manager**: Reviews risk register, coordinates mitigation plans, flags compliance blockers
- **With Risk Manager** (if separate): Collaborates on enterprise risk assessment
- **With Developers**: Advises on technical compliance requirements (security, data handling, etc.)
- **With Stakeholder Liaison**: Communicates compliance implications to stakeholders

### Escalation Path
- Routine compliance questions → Project Manager
- High-impact risks → Project Manager + Executive Sponsor
- Regulatory violations or critical compliance gaps → Executive Leadership + Legal

### Typical Activities
- Performing compliance gap analyses
- Creating risk assessments and mitigation plans
- Maintaining compliance checklists and tracking
- Reviewing security and data privacy implementations
- Conducting compliance audits
- Documenting compliance decisions and approvals

## Cross-Functional Coordinator

### Role Summary
The Cross-Functional Coordinator facilitates collaboration and coordination across multiple teams and departments working on shared work streams. This role ensures dependencies are managed and communication flows smoothly between siloed teams.

### Responsibilities
- Map and track cross-team dependencies
- Facilitate coordination meetings between teams
- Identify and resolve inter-team blockers and conflicts
- Maintain dependency tracking and status reporting
- Escalate cross-functional bottlenecks to leadership
- Coordinate resource sharing and prioritization disputes
- Document handoffs and touchpoints between teams

### Goals
- Minimize delays caused by cross-team dependencies
- Improve collaboration and reduce silos across departments
- Ensure clear communication of priorities across teams
- Enable faster issue resolution through proactive coordination

### Key Interactions
- **With Project Manager**: Reports coordination status, escalates blockers, aligns team priorities
- **With Multiple Delivery Teams**: Coordinates schedules, manages handoffs, resolves conflicts
- **With Product Manager**: Aligns feature dependencies and sequencing
- **With Stakeholder Liaison**: Communicates cross-team impacts to stakeholders

### Escalation Path
- Standard dependency coordination → Project Manager
- Resource allocation conflicts → Project Manager + Department Leads
- Priority misalignment → Product Manager + Department Leads

### Typical Activities
- Hosting dependency mapping sessions
- Maintaining cross-functional timeline and roadmap
- Facilitating integration and handoff reviews
- Tracking and reporting on cross-team bottlenecks
- Coordinating shared resource allocation
- Documenting and communicating sequence dependencies

---

## Knowledge Management Owner

### Role Summary
The Knowledge Management Owner captures, documents, and preserves project learnings, best practices, and institutional knowledge. This role ensures continuous improvement through retrospectives and enables knowledge transfer across teams.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate retrospectives and lessons learned sessions
- Document and codify best practices and process improvements
- Maintain project documentation and knowledge base
- Ensure knowledge transfer and onboarding materials are current
- Archive project artifacts and lessons learned
- Identify and promote reusable processes and templates
- Create and maintain team playbooks and runbooks

### Goals
- Capture and preserve institutional knowledge for future projects
- Enable faster onboarding and ramp-up of new team members
- Improve process maturity through documented learnings
- Reduce rework by leveraging past experiences and best practices

### Key Interactions
- **With Project Manager**: Coordinates retrospectives, captures action items, documents outcomes
- **With All Team Members**: Gathers input on lessons learned and process improvements
- **With Scrum Master** (if present): Supports team reflection and continuous improvement ceremonies
- **With Stakeholder Liaison**: Documents stakeholder feedback and learnings

### Escalation Path
- Standard documentation → Project Manager
- Process improvement recommendations → Project Manager + Relevant Team Leads
- Critical organizational learnings → Project Manager + Executive Sponsor

### Typical Activities
- Planning and facilitating retrospective meetings
- Documenting lessons learned and action items
- Creating process improvement templates and checklists
- Maintaining project knowledge repositories
- Training team members on processes and procedures
- Creating and updating runbooks and playbooks
- Archiving and organizing project artifacts
